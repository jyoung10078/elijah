RESEARCH REPORT: THE CURRENT STATE OF THE HEALTHCARE SYSTEM IN THE UNITED STATES

1. OVERVIEW
- The U.S. healthcare system is one of the largest and most complex in the world.
- It is largely provided by private entities, with a mix of public programs (Medicare, Medicaid).
- In 2024, 92% of Americans (about 310 million people) had health insurance for some or all of the year.
- Private health insurance: 66.1% | Public coverage: 35.5%

2. HEALTHCARE SPENDING
- U.S. health care spending in 2024: $5.3 trillion (18% of GDP).
- Health spending increased by 7.2% from 2023 to 2024.
- In 2023, health spending was 17.6% of GDP (~$4.9 trillion).
- National health expenditures projected to grow 8.2% in 2024 and 7.1% in 2025.
- Physician and clinical services accounted for $1.1 trillion (21%) of spending in 2024.
- By comparison, in 2000, health expenditures were ~$1.4 trillion.

3. INSURANCE COVERAGE & ACCESS
- 8% of Americans (about 26 million) remain uninsured.
- 10 states have not fully expanded Medicaid under the ACA.
- Texas has the highest uninsured rate (16.6%); Massachusetts the lowest (2.4%).
- Hispanic Americans have the highest uninsured rates and the most cost-related barriers to care.

4. HEALTHCARE WORKFORCE CHALLENGES
- The U.S. faces growing healthcare workforce shortages driven by maldistribution of providers, burnout, and systemic issues.
- More than 122 million people lived in a Mental Health Professional Shortage Area as of December 2024.
- Physician shortages are concentrated in rural and underserved urban areas.
- Burnout, secondary trauma, and compassion fatigue are major issues across the workforce.

5. RACIAL & SOCIOECONOMIC DISPARITIES
- White populations generally receive better quality care compared to Black, Hispanic, AIAN, and AANHPI communities.
- Nonelderly AIAN, Black, Hispanic, and NHPI people are more likely to be uninsured than White counterparts.
- Unequal access to primary care is a key driver of disparities in healthcare quality.
- Hispanic people have lower premature mortality rates despite higher uninsured rates.

- Behavioral healthcare faces financial, structural, and policy barriers to quality access.
- Mental health parity remains an ongoing legislative and policy issue.

7. TECHNOLOGY & INNOVATION
- 71.4% of physicians reported using telehealth weekly in 2024, up from 25.1% in 2018.
- AI in U.S. healthcare market estimated at $13.26 billion in 2024, projected to grow at 36.76% CAGR.
- Generative AI solutions are being rapidly adopted across healthcare organizations.
- AI is improving diagnostics, treatment decisions, and patient care delivery.
- Telehealth has become a mainstream channel post-pandemic.

8. KEY CHALLENGES
- Rising costs far exceed inflation and GDP growth.
- Significant geographic, racial, and socioeconomic disparities in access and quality.
- Workforce shortages, especially in mental health and rural care.
- Ongoing debates around ACA sustainability, Medicaid expansion, and pharmaceutical pricing.
- Post-pandemic stress on the healthcare system structure and workforce.

9. POLICY LANDSCAPE
- The Affordable Care Act (ACA) remains central to coverage expansion.
- Medicaid expansion still incomplete in 10 states.
- Debates on drug pricing, surprise medical billing, and mental health parity continue.
- Maternal mortality rates rising, with new policy restrictions adding pressure.
